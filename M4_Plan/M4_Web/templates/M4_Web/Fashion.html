Fashion: Textiles, Apparel & Engineering

Completely integrated solutions for the Fashion industry

There is at present severe competition between leading industry players and new ventures planning to create a niche in the global Textiles & Apparel (T&A) industry. At Technopak, our objective is to assist T&A organizations in optimizing their profits through the enhancement and expansion of their various industry-centric sectors.

With about two decades of experience in delivering end-to-end solutions to the entire gamut of the textiles industry, right from fiber to retailing, the Fashion sector at Technopak continues to be a core business unit of our organization.

Our market-driven approach allows retailers and brands to improve their performance in this fast-changing business environment. Many leading Indian and international Textiles and Apparel manufacturers and brands have benefited from our service offerings in the areas of business planning and strategy, apparel operations, supply chain management, and strategic alliances.

Today, we are positioned as the leading consultants in the textiles domain thanks to our strong and reliable relationship with our clients. Our team consists of top caliber advisors who have worked closely with a diverse group of clients including textiles manufacturers, apparel and brand retailers, garment manufacturers and exporters, apparel sourcing organizations, trade promotion councils, industry associations, international development bodies, financial institutions, and central and state governments.

The Future of Cotton

Cotton has always been a fiber of substantial economic impact as well as the backbone of textiles industry from time immemorial. The initial waves of industrialization evolved around the cotton textiles industry which heralded a new era of economic development. However, after the invention of manmade fibers which provided scope for maneuvering fiber properties to suit specific end-use requirements, the share of cotton fiber in textiles consumption has decline over the years, a trend expected to continue in the future as well. But cotton is not going to lose its impact as a fiber of choice in the fashion value chain; rather, it will gradually gain the status of a premier fiber for high-end fashion applications. China, India, Pakistan, and the United States are expected to remain the major cotton-producing countries in the shortto medium-term. The first three of these countries will increasingly play a dominant role in both cotton production as well as the cotton textiles industry. The branding of cotton, increased focus on the sustainable cotton value chain, the introduction of cotton-specific programs by brands and retailers, and heightened consumer awareness have made the global cotton, and cotton textiles, industry more vibrant and dynamic. India being among the leading cotton fiber and cotton textiles producers is well positioned to leverage its reputation as a cotton textile sourcing hub to strengthen its global image.
